Core Concept & Content
▶ What Parents Will Experience PARENT JOURNEY

9:00 AM: Arrival and Welcome

Parents arrive at one of three welcome tents (FOD, Gym, or EBF entrance). They receive a welcome packet with bunk information, counselor bios, printed unit photos, suggested questions to ask their kid, the day's schedule, and a camp map. Printed welcome banners greet them at the entrances. Staff is welcoming and oriented. They head straight to their child's bunk.

At the Bunk

Their child is there, ready and excited. Counselors greet parents, introduce themselves, and give a quick bunk tour. This is the counselor trust-building moment. Parents meet the people caring for their kid in the kid's actual living space. This sets the tone for the entire visit.

10:30 AM: Waterfront, Studio, Woods Open

All three activity areas are open. Parents and kids explore camp together. They might go tie-dye, climb the rock wall, swim, play pickleball, or just walk around the Hollow. It's their choice. This is family exploration time.

12:30 PM: BBQ Lunch and Portraits

Families head to lunch. BBQ buffet is served. Groups called up by unit. While eating, they can stop for family portraits near the dining hall. A photographer (Elliott or assigned staff) captures the moment with a K&E branded backdrop.

1:15 PM: Jack's Announcements

Jack speaks for 3-5 minutes. He thanks the parents, celebrates the summer, and previews what's next. He mentions that activities reopen, portraits continue, DL meetings are starting right now in the dining hall, and departure is at 3:00. Short and real.

1:20 PM: DL Meetings + Hut 6/Juniper Parent Meeting

Feeds directly from Jack's announcements. DLs sit at tables in the Dining Hall with signs above their heads for each division. Parents can sit with their kid's DL, ask questions, meet other families in the division. Hut 6 and Juniper parent meeting happens simultaneously in the same space. Casual, open format. Come and go as you like.

1:30 PM: Activities Reopen

Waterfront, Studio, and Woods open again. Same setup as 10:30. Portraits continue until 2:30. It's a flexible afternoon.

3:00 PM: Goodbye and Departure

Announcements go out: "It's time to say goodbye. Counselors, please take your campers back to the bunk." Parents walk to the parking lot. Kids go with counselors. Clean break. No lingering. The goodbye is the hardest part. A structured, clear departure means less pain.

Evening: Post-Departure

Kids go back to bunks with counselors for chill time. A planned activity from 3:30-5:30 PM. Dinner at 5:30. Free play in the Hollow and compounds. Movie night in the gym. Something fun to ease the re-separation.

▶ What's Different This Year KEY SHIFTS

1. Parents Go Straight to Bunks (9:00 AM)

In 2025, parents watched activities first. In 2026, kids are waiting at the bunk. Counselors are there. The first moment is a family moment, not a spectator moment. Parent feedback said new families felt lost. Industry research says the number one thing parents want is quality time with their kid. This gives it to them immediately.

2. Waterfront, Studio, Woods Open (not activities)

At 10:30 AM and 1:30 PM, we list specific activity areas instead of generic "activities open." All areas are open and available for families to choose. Studio (tie-dye, crafts, ceramics), Woods (high ropes, rock wall, archery, pickleball), Waterfront (swimming, inflatable, canoeing, SUP), and free play areas (Hollow, compounds, fields).

3. Welcome Packets with Counselor Info

Instead of a generic printed info sheet, packets include bunk details, counselor bios, unit photos from the first three weeks, and suggested questions to ask their kid. This helps new families feel less lost and gives them conversation starters.

4. DL Meetings Right After Announcements (1:20 PM)

New for 2026. Jack announces at 1:15. DL meetings start immediately at 1:20, feeding right in. Division leaders sit in the Dining Hall with signs above their heads. Parents can sit with their kid's DL, ask questions, get oriented. Hut 6 and Juniper (third-year seniors) have their own parent meeting simultaneously. More accessible than a formal presentation.

5. Departure at 3:00 PM

30 minutes earlier than 2025. Industry research says the goodbye is the hardest part. A cleaner, earlier departure means less lingering and less pain. Counselors take kids back to bunks at 3:00. Clean break.

6. Post-Departure Activity 3:30-5:30 PM

Something planned for the evening. A fun activity to ease re-separation after parents leave. Then dinner at 5:30, free play, and movie night in the gym. Industry best practice is a big activity after parents leave. This eases the transition for kids.

7. Dave Departs 8:30 AM

Non-visitor kids (~15) go with Adventure Dave before parents arrive. Hike, ice cream, lake excursion. They get something exclusively theirs. They feel special, not left behind.

8. Welcome Banners at Gates

Mariel designs and produces printed welcome banners at each entrance and branded backdrop for family portraits. Not just signs. Real banners to set the tone and make it feel festive. Geof moves extra picnic tables to the assembly area for lunch.

▶ Special Situations EXCEPTIONS & CARE

Kids Without Visiting Parents (~15)

Adventure Dave takes non-visitor kids on a trip off-campus. Hike, ice cream run, lake excursion. They get something exclusively theirs. They feel special, not left behind. Dave needs a plan and supplies. Departure at 8:30 AM, before gates open.

Rookie Week Overlap

Rookie campers with full-season siblings can stay overnight Friday and get picked up Saturday morning during Visiting Day. Standard Rookie pickup is Friday July 17 at 2 PM. This allows new families to experience the community and see their sibling on Visiting Day.

Friday Night: Pre-Visiting Day

Suggest a spot in the parent info sheet. Something like: "Many families gather Friday evening at [restaurant]. No RSVP needed, just show up if you're in town." Zero camp staff effort. Pick the specific restaurant closer to summer.
