9 events in 2024, 17 in 2026. Rebuilt an entirely new 5-camp partner network after losing RobinHood and Birchmont. Impressive growth, but composition is skewed toward U15 and all-ages.

Key Finding: "All Ages" is doing heavy lifting for younger girls

▶

6 of 17 Girls 2026 events are "All Ages" (Dance x2, Swim x2, Gymnastics, Archery). Jack confirmed these genuinely work for younger campers, so they count. But it means U12 girls' entire competitive calendar beyond Basketball comes from these shared events. No dedicated soccer, tennis, flag football, or volleyball for U12.

Key Finding: Boys program is mature and stable

▶

Same 4-camp network for 3 years running (K&E, Winaukee, RobinHood, Birchmont). Event count steady at 17-19. Good age group coverage with dedicated events for U10 through U15. The main concern is U10 losing coverage in 2026.

Context: Girls lost their camp partners

▶

The girls' camp network went from K&E + RobinHood + Birchmont (2024-2025) to K&E + Walt Whitman + Robindel + Wico + Merriwood (2026). This was not by choice. The 2026 schedule being this full with brand-new partners is a strong operational signal, but it means less institutional history and more coordination unknowns.

Recommendations

Actionable next steps based on the analysis

1. Add 1-2 dedicated Girls U12 events
U12 is the most underserved group. 1 dedicated event, 0 shared bracket events, and no dedicated ball sports beyond basketball. Adding U12 Soccer and/or Tennis would close the biggest gap in the entire program.
Talk to Walt Whitman or Robindel about hosting a U12 Soccer event in the 7/14-7/20 window.
2. Restore Boys U10 coverage
U10 boys had Soccer and Baseball in 2024. Both are gone in 2026, leaving them with just Swimming and Archery. This is a regression.
Winaukee hosted U10 Baseball in 2024. Ask if they can again. The 7/18-7/23 gap in the schedule has room.
3. Reconsider the "15/13/11" bracket structure
The bracket naming explicitly excludes U12. If K&E has a U12 division, these kids are the only age group left out of both dedicated AND shared bracket events. Could be a "15/13/12/11" bracket instead.
Confirm whether U12 exclusion is structural (no U12 division) or an oversight. If it's structural, the gap analysis changes.
4. Understand and address K&E opt-outs
K&E skips the 15/13/11 Tennis at Wico and the All Swim on 7/24. If these are travel distance or scheduling issues, they may be fixable. If they're choices, younger age groups lose disproportionately.
Map the reasons for each opt-out. See if alternate dates or car-pool coordination could change any.
5. Fill the Boys 2026 schedule gaps
The Boys 2026 sheet has a daily calendar format with empty rows after 7/17 until 7/24. Several days with no events. May still be in progress.
Confirm finalization status with the boys' scheduling lead. The gap could accommodate additional U10 or U12 events.
6. Track sport variety equity year over year
Right now, U15 girls access 9 different sports while U12 girls access 5. Making this visible each year prevents drift.
Add a "Sports Available" column to the master schedule by age group. One row per group, checkmarks per sport. Makes the gap obvious at a glance.
